## SLIDE PRESENTATION DELIVERY GUIDE

### McKinsey-Style Problem-First Approach

**Presentation Flow & Timing:**
- **Total Presentation:** 25-30 minutes
- **Discovery First:** Ask questions before presenting (Phase 1)
- **Problem Phase (Slides 2-5):** 10-12 minutes - Lead with business challenges
- **Solution Phase (Slides 6-10):** 10-12 minutes - Present how we solve it
- **Path Forward (Slides 11-14):** 5-6 minutes - Pilot and next steps
- **Interactive:** Pause for questions, make it conversational, validate problems

## KEY DELIVERY TIPS - PROBLEM PHASE (CBO/CMO Lens)

### When Presenting Slide 2 (Business Challenge):
- **Start with Research:** "McKinsey research shows 8 in 10 organizations see no bottom-line gains from AI"
- **Make It Personal:** "Let's validate - what's your current lead-to-enrolment conversion rate?"
- **Frame as Revenue Problem:** "This isn't a technology problem - it's a revenue efficiency problem"
- **Ask:** "What's your biggest pain point: lead quality, counsellor productivity, or discoverability?"

### When Presenting Slide 3 (Current State - Revenue Impact):
- **Validate Metrics:** "Let's quantify the problem - what's your current CPE? Sales cycle time?"
- **Show Business Impact:** "Every day of delay = lost enrolments = revenue leakage"
- **Make It Visual:** Use their actual numbers if possible
- **Ask:** "What would a 20% improvement in conversion mean for your business?"

### When Presenting Slide 4 (Root Cause Analysis):
- **Don't Blame Tools:** "The issue isn't your tools - it's that workflows need end-to-end redesign"
- **Show the Gap:** "Bolt-on AI vs. reimagined workflows - that's the difference"
- **Emphasize:** "AI needs to embed reasoning into decisions, not just assist"
- **Ask:** "Where do you see the biggest bottlenecks in your current process?"

### When Presenting Slide 5 (Opportunity Cost):
- **Market Context:** "Students are shifting to AI search - are you visible there?"
- **Competitive Urgency:** "Early movers win compounding visibility - the window is closing"
- **Quantify Potential:** "Research shows 4-7 point pipeline lift, 10-20% cycle time reduction"
- **Ask:** "What's the cost of inaction? What are competitors doing?"

1. **Validate Problems First:** Don't jump to solutions until they agree on problems
2. **Use Their Numbers:** Ask for metrics, then show how you improve them
3. **Frame as Business Outcomes:** Revenue, efficiency, margins - not technology features
4. **Show Research Credibility:** Reference McKinsey, industry benchmarks
5. **Make It Conversational:** Pause, ask questions, validate assumptions
6. **Focus on ROI:** Always tie back to business impact, not just capabilities
