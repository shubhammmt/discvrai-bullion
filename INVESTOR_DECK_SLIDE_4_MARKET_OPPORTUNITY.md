## The Bottom-Up Story: Starting with Rahul

### Meet Rahul (LAMF Persona)

**Profile:**
- 35-year-old IT professional in Bangalore
- Annual income: ₹28 lakh
- MF portfolio: ₹18 lakh (running 3 SIPs for 4 years)
- UPI power user (PhonePe), tracks investments weekly

**Pain Point:**
- Needs ₹8 lakh for daughter's education (emergency fund gap)
- Doesn't want to break SIPs (tax + market timing risk)
- Traditional bank loans require 2-3 weeks, high documentation

**Rahul's Journey with Discvr:**

1. **Discovery** (Week 1): Reads article "Should You Break Your SIP for Emergency Expenses?" on Discvr feed
2. **Education** (Week 1-2): Uses Discvr's "EMI vs. Pledge Calculator", compares LAMF vs. personal loan
3. **Conversion** (Week 3): Applies for ₹8L LAMF via Discvr partner, approved in 48 hours
4. **Engagement** (Ongoing): Returns weekly for MF news, shares calculator with 3 colleagues

**Unit Economics:**
- **Transaction**: ₹8 lakh LAMF disbursement
- **Discvr Commission**: 0.6% = ₹4,800 revenue
- **CAC**: ₹0 (organic SEO + content)
- **Gross Margin**: 100% (commission-based model)

**Key Insight**: One Rahul = ₹4,800 revenue at zero CAC. The magic is in scale.

## Discussion: Do We Need Separate Stories for Gold & Silver?

### Option A: Unified "Precious Metals" Story (Simpler)

**Pros**:
- Cleaner narrative (2 personas vs. 3)
- Gold + silver share 80% overlap (UPI users, inflation hedge, SIP model)
- Investor focus on category, not sub-category

**Cons**:
- Loses silver's unique positioning (lower entry, industrial demand)
- Misses opportunity to show market depth

**Recommendation for Slide**: Combine gold + silver into "Digital Precious Metals" (₹8.92 cr revenue Y1-2), use Priya as representative persona

---

### Option B: Keep Separate Stories (More Detailed)

**Pros**:
- Shows market sophistication (gold for wealth, silver for volume)
- Highlights silver's 18% CAGR (fastest growing)
- Demonstrates platform flexibility (multiple user cohorts)

**Cons**:
- More complex deck (3 personas vs. 2)
- Risk of over-detailing in early slides

**Recommendation for Appendix**: Keep detailed breakdown in backup slides, simplify main deck
