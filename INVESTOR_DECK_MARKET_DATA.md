## Detailed Market Sizing (Commission-Based Revenue)

### Loan Against Mutual Funds
Year 1 revenue ₹0.86 cr (0.6% commission on ₹144 cr loans); Year 2 revenue ₹3.14 cr (commission on ₹524 cr). Ticket sizes stay in the ₹6-₹6.5L range while cohorts are fixed. From Year 4 onward, user cohorts expand with the 12% market CAGR, driving to ₹27.69 cr (Year 5) and ₹153.57 cr (Year 10).

### Digital Gold
Year 1 revenue ₹0.21 cr (2% commission, ~1g purchase per user); Year 2 revenue ₹1.55 cr (2% commission, ~2g per user). Cohort stays at 3M for the first three years, then expands with 15% market CAGR, delivering ₹33.30 cr (Year 5) and ₹399.34 cr (Year 10).

### Digital Silver
Year 1 revenue ₹0.06 cr (2% commission, 100g per user); Year 2 revenue ₹0.35 cr (2% commission, 150g per user). Cohort held at 1M through Year 3, expands with 18% market CAGR from Year 4, leading to ₹8.94 cr (Year 5) and ₹130.66 cr (Year 10).

**Key Conservative Assumptions**: We prove PMF on fixed cohorts in Years 1-3 (penetration 0.2-0.8%, modest ticket sizes), then layer in market-driven cohort expansion from Year 4 onward. Annual revenue starts at ₹1.14 cr (Year 1) and reaches ₹5.04 cr (Year 2) before the market flywheel accelerates. All figures reflect commission revenue (not transaction value). ---

### Product-Specific Targets:

#### LAMF Beachhead (Year 1-2)
**Target**: 1.2M salaried/HNI investors in top-8 metros with >₹10L MF holdings, aged 30–50, already running SIPs; pain = liquidity without exit.

**Conservative Target**: 0.2% (Y1) → 0.6% (Y2) penetration = 2,400 → 7,200 users.

**Revenue**: ₹0.86 cr (Y1) → ₹3.14 cr (Y2 cumulative) = ₹4.00 cr total over two years (0.6% commission on ₹668 cr cumulative transaction value).

**Strategy**: Lower ticket size (₹6-6.5L vs ₹8L industry avg). Focus on proving PMF first. On-ramp through "portfolio doctor" content, EMI vs. pledge calculators.

**Market context**: 9.25 crore active SIP accounts, ₹29,361 crore monthly SIP (Sep 2025).[3][4]

---

#### Digital Gold Beachhead (Year 1-2)
**Target**: 3M UPI power users (PhonePe/Google Pay) aged 23–35 in Tier-1/2 cities, monthly disposable ₹25–75k, high festive spenders.

**Conservative Target**: 0.3% (Y1) → 0.8% (Y2) adoption = 9,000 → 27,600 users.

**Revenue**: ₹0.21 cr (Y1) → ₹1.55 cr (Y2 cumulative) = ₹1.76 cr total over two years (2% commission on ₹88 cr cumulative transaction value).

**Strategy**: Lower purchase volume (1–2g/year vs 15g target). Hook with "₹100/day gold challenge," gifting kits, wealth drip emails.

**Key levers**: Low entry (₹1-₹10), SIPs (₹5k/month = ~5g), festival/gifting spikes.

**Gold price**: ₹11,849/g (Nov'25).[5][6][10]

---

#### Digital Silver Beachhead (Year 1-2)
**Target**: 1M addressable users (kept constant for first three years), age 25-40, tech-savvy investors.

**Conservative Target**: 0.2% (Y1) → 0.5% (Y2) adoption = 2,000 → 5,900 users.

**Revenue**: ₹0.06 cr (Y1) → ₹0.35 cr (Y2 cumulative) = ₹0.35 cr total over two years (2% commission on ₹17 cr cumulative transaction value).

**Strategy**: Lower volume (100-150g/year vs 200g target).

**Key levers**: More affordable than gold (₹160/g), SIP model (₹3k/month = ~41g), industrial + investment demand.

**Silver ETFs**: ₹13,500 cr AUM, strong inflows.[6]
