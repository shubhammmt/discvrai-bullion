## Focused Target Segments - Year 1-2 Niche TG

### Primary TG: "Affluent Urban Professionals"

**Demographics:**
- **Location**: Top-8 cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune, Kolkata, Ahmedabad)
- **Age**: 28-45 years
- **Income**: ₹12-50 lakh annually
- **Occupation**: Salaried professionals, business owners, HNIs

**Financial Profile:**
- MF holdings: >₹10 lakh
- Active SIP investors
- UPI power users (PhonePe/Google Pay)
- Tech-savvy, financially literate
- **Size**: ~1.5-2M individuals

### Product-Specific Targets:

#### LAMF Beachhead (Year 1-2)
1.2M salaried/HNI investors in top-8 metros with >₹10L MF holdings, aged 30–50, already running SIPs; pain = liquidity without exit. 

**Conservative Target**: 0.2% (Y1) → 0.6% (Y2) penetration = 2,400 → 7,200 users. 

**Revenue**: ₹0.86 cr (Y1) → ₹2.81 cr (Y2) = ₹3.67 cr total (0.6% commission on ₹612 cr transaction value). 

Lower ticket size (₹6-6.5L vs ₹8L industry avg). Focus on proving PMF first. On-ramp through "portfolio doctor" content, EMI vs. pledge calculators. 

**Market context**: 9.25 crore active SIP accounts, ₹29,361 crore monthly SIP (Sep 2025).[3][4]

#### Digital Gold Beachhead (Year 1-2)
3M UPI power users (PhonePe/Google Pay) aged 23–35 in Tier-1/2 cities, monthly disposable ₹25–75k, high festive spenders. 

**Conservative Target**: 0.3% (Y1) → 0.8% (Y2) adoption = 9,000 → 24,000 users. 

**Revenue**: ₹1.71 cr (Y1) → ₹5.69 cr (Y2) = ₹7.4 cr total (2% commission on ₹368 cr transaction value). 

Lower purchase volume (8-10g/year vs 15g target). Hook with "₹100/day gold challenge," gifting kits, wealth drip emails. 

**Key levers**: Low entry (₹1-₹10), SIPs (₹5k/month = ~5g), festival/gifting spikes. Gold price: ₹11,849/g (Nov'25).[5][6][10]

#### Digital Silver Beachhead (Year 1-2)
5M addressable users (overlaps with gold + new segment seeking lower entry point), age 25-40, tech-savvy investors. 

**Conservative Target**: 0.2% (Y1) → 0.5% (Y2) adoption = 10,000 → 25,000 users. 

**Revenue**: ₹0.32 cr (Y1) → ₹1.2 cr (Y2) = ₹1.52 cr total (2% commission on ₹416 cr transaction value). 

Lower volume (100-150g/year vs 200g target). 

**Key levers**: More affordable than gold (₹160/g), SIP model (₹3k/month = ~41g), industrial + investment demand. Silver ETFs: ₹13,500 cr AUM, strong inflows.[6]

**Total Year 1-2 Conservative Targets**: ~21K users (Y1) → ~56K users (Y2), ₹12.59 cr revenue (commission-based). Focus on quality over quantity, proving PMF before scaling.
