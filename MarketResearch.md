Revenue Model & Monetization
We plan a “Platform + Intelligence + Services” model:
• Subscription (SaaS): Freemium tier with basic aggregation. Premium at ~₹999/month (₹11.9k/yr) unlocks advanced analytics, unlimited integrations and insights. (By comparison, India’s Moneycontrol Pro app charges ~₹999/year for premium content 15 .) We will validate this price through user surveys and A/B tests, but early indicators suggest tech-savvy urban users will tolerate ~₹10–12k/yr for truly valuable finance tools. At a 15% premium-conversion rate, this yields ~₹1,800/ user/yr from subscriptions.
• Referral Commissions: When users take financial products via our platform, we earn standard industry commissions. For example: life-insurance referrals can yield ~15–30% of first-year premium 16 (IRDAI rules allow up to 100% on term-life and ~30% on general/health insurance 16 ); mutual- fund leads typically earn ~0.15–1.95% trail fees 17 ; personal loans/credit cards ~₹2,000–5,000 per referral (e.g. MoneyView’s affiliate is 1.5% of loan 18 ). We conservatively project ~₹2,400/user/yr from such commissions by year 1. Notably, INDmoney’s FY24 showed 76% of its revenue (₹53.6 Cr)
from distribution services (referrals) 11 .
• Premium Services: On top of core features, we will offer high-value add-ons: e.g. advanced AI advisory ($3,000/yr), tax optimization ($5,000/yr), or personalized financial planning ($9,999/yr). These target <5% of power users (e.g. HNIs) but command high fees. Such service rates are in line with financial consultants’ fees for year-long guidance. We estimate these yield another ~₹1,200/ user/yr at scale (assuming a small percentage upsell).
Together, these streams target a blended ARPU of ~₹5,400/user/yr by end of Year 1 (Premium ₹1.8k + Commissions ₹2.4k + Services ₹1.2k). We will continuously refine pricing: for example, Moneycontrol and ETMoney have demonstrated user willingness to pay in the low-₹1k/yr range for premium finance tools. We will also explore periodic discounts/annual pricing to boost conversion.
Each revenue line will be tracked for profitability. For instance, CAC vs. LTV (target >8x) will be monitored; currently, lower-end fintech acquisitions run ₹2–3k per user, which our premium lifetime (~₹16.2k over 3 yrs) comfortably covers. Should user WTP tests show ₹999/m too high, we can adjust tiers (e.g. offer ₹499/m or bundling with financial products). The diverse mix (subscription + commissions + services) de-risks reliance on any single source and mirrors models of peers like INDmoney (subscription+distribution) 11 and global standards.
